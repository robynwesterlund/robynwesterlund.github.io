Robyn's Violin Studio

Robyn Westerlund · Austin, TX · (555) 555-5555 · robyn.westerlund@gmail.com

Robyn Sato has played the violin for over 20 years, beginning at 3 years old. Her first teachers included Suzuki violin teachers Deanna Badgett, and Dr. Tammy Linn. As a young student, Robyn participated in a variety of orchestras and chamber ensembles, and she also regularly competed in her districts’ Solo and Ensemble competitions. In her high school years, Robyn began teaching violin as a volunteer at the Austin Korean School. Her experiences teaching led Robyn to choose Music Education as her course of study at Texas State University. Her violin professor, Paula Bird, further shaped Robyn as a violinist and encouraged her to pursue Suzuki Violin teacher training. Robyn received training in violin pedagogy under Dr. Laurie Scott, Nancy Lokken, Paule Barsalou, and Mark Mutter for Suzuki Violin books 1-4 respectively. Robyn also spent a year abroad in Tokyo, Japan where she changed her degree plan to International Studies with a music minor while continuing her violin study under professional violinist Mami Ichikawa. Robyn graduated from Texas State University in 2017 with a Bachelor’s Degree in International Studies and minors in Music and Japanese. Robyn has been teaching for over 4 years, and maintains a violin studio in South Austin, Texas. Robyn also enjoys gardening, studying Japanese, and exploring Austin with her husband Katz, and miniature poodle, Yuki.

Getting Started

Learning how to play the violin is for everyone! My teaching philosophy supports the idea that people at any age or ability can learn to make music whether they are interested in lessons for themselves or for their child. I strive to create an environment of encouragement and support so that my students can develop not only as musicians but in other areas of life including confidence, self-discipline, concentration and patience.

Course Of Study for Children

My studio emphasizes music making in two important ways, as a solo musician, and as member of a group. My studio requires that students under the age of 18 that are not participating in an outside orchestra or group playing organization participate in bi-monthly group lessons in addition to their weekly private lessons. This encourages musical development and provides a well-rounded musical education for students regardless of their goals. For students age 4 or younger, the plan is slightly altered. Please contact me for more information on very young students starting the violin.

Course of Study for Adults

Adults are most welcome in my studio! Adults can choose between weekly or bi-monthly lessons. These lessons are only scheduled between the hours of 7-8 P.M. on Monday, Tuesday, and Thursday. A monthly “Music and Dessert” will be held for free if enough adults are able to make it. These are casual play-ins where adults can experience playing together as a group and that is one of the best aspects of learning a musical instrument!
